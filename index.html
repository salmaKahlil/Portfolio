About

Salma Khalil '25, living in Egypt, graduated as the top-ranked student from her STEM school and secured UAMA full scholarship to support her entire university education. She is a third-year student at the Amercian University in Cairo (AUC) where she is pursuing a degree in computer science (CS) and a minor in business administration with an overall GPA of 3.625. She serves as a mentor in Google Developers Student Clubs, leveraging her expertise to assist students in excelling at website development from the ground up. She is actively involved with the Computer Science and Engineering Association, dedicated to enhancing students' experiences and facilitating their academic progress. As a volunteer in Resala, she advocates for children rights and leads visits to schools and orphanages. She currently pursues her studies oversees at the SUNY New Paltz located in New York; She benefits from the culture and technical exchange gained after such enriching experience. Upon finishing her undergraduate studies, she plans to aquire hands-on experiences as a software engineer.
